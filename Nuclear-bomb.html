Detonation methods and elemental isotopes

The two main detonation methods are Gun type and implosion type detonation methods (see diagrams below)

The two bombs I will talk about in specific are fat man and little boy which are the one that were dropped on Japan in 1945.
Little boy used Uranium isotope U-235 and the gun type detonation method. This means that a highly reactive or explosive chemical element get rapidly shot into a 2 peice U-235 core which then collides the two together and kickstarts the fission reaction (refer to section below for more information). On the other hand Fat man utilizes the implosion detonation method which uses elecromagnetic waves and signals to bbegin a chemical reaction in the outer rings of reactive chemical elements then generate so much heat and pressure that it pushes the plutonium isoptope Pu-239 core more and more while the inside of the core is doing the same due to the nuclear fission reaction until it eventually implodes on itself and releases all of its force and energy.

Why did they use 2 types of elemental cores?

At first the they used Uranium 235 in the original little boy but they realised that it was unethical and extremely difficult to atttian a critical amount of U-235 as they had to work endlessly to seperate it from its already uncommon isotopes U-239 and U-238. They then found out that they can use a plutonium core instead specifically Pu-239 which is not a natural isotope and can efficiently be made in a nclear reactor which provides a simpler and more stable source of the element.






Nuclear fission


Nuclear fission is a chain reaction that releases energy


The diagram you see is a chain reaction that causes nuclear bombs to detonate.
How it works is a rogue neutron gets rapidly shot into an atom in this case the atom is a U-235 atom. The neutron shoots straight through the atom and
splits its nucleus theoretically in 'half' but that has not been proven. This splitting of the nucleas causes three outcomes:

1. It splits the atom into two smaller atoms
2. It releases an imense amount of energy
3. It spews out 1-3 neutrons from it s nucleus

The splitting of the nucleus of the atom turning it into two lighter nuclei and from the tearing and interuption of a nuclear force come a rather large
burst of energy. The nucleus, in the proccess of tearing releases 1-3 neutrons which then rapidly fly out to other nearby atoms. One neutron may fuse
with a nearby atom, another may just miss an atom entirely and float around until it finds a nucleus to fuse with, the final one may strike through another atom and
split it s nucleus which causes another fission reaction and continues the chain reaction.
